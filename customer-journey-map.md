# Customer Journey Map

## Public Site

### 1. Offer

Goal: establish the proposition quickly.

The visitor should understand that this is a human coaching service for AI-era professional transition.

Desired feeling: serious, relevant, calm and worth exploring.

### 2. Human Value

Goal: explain why coaching is not replaced by AI.

The section should make clear that AI can support preparation, but not confidentiality, trust, challenge, context-sensitive judgement or accountability.

### 3. Role-Based Snapshot

Goal: make the visitor feel recognised.

The archetype selector should be compact, elegant and easy to scan. The questions should feel mature and professionally specific.

The assessment should not feel like a generic quiz.

### 4. Result State

Goal: create useful self-recognition and a reason to continue.

The result should feel specific enough to be valuable, but incomplete enough to make the paid Deep Navigation Scan a logical next step.

### 5. Paid Deep Navigation Scan

Goal: show what becomes more precise in the paid offer.

The preview should communicate:

- deeper diagnostic modules
- AI Navigation Brief
- sample tool output
- connection to coaching journey

The paid scan should not appear to replace coaching. It prepares the human conversation.

### 6. Coaching Journey

Goal: show the path from insight to behaviour change.

The six journeys should feel like a practical development path, not a course catalogue.

### 7. Booking

Goal: create a simple, credible next step.

The booking section should feel private, low-friction and appropriate for a senior professional.

## Reviewer Workspace

### 1. Reviewer Entry

Goal: make clear that this is a private testing area.

The reviewer should understand that the live site and review tasks are separate.

### 2. Task Flow

Goal: guide market testers through the intended sequence.

The current sequence:

1. Experience the live offer
2. Take the free snapshot
3. Inspect the paid preview
4. Price the paid path

### 3. Evidence Pack

Goal: let testers judge the paid offer with enough context.

The evidence pack should preview the kind of output a client might receive without giving away the whole method.

### 4. Pricing Feedback

Goal: test willingness to pay and what would increase trust.

The form should feel clear and quick, not like a long research survey.
