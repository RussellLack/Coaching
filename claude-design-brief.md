# Claude Design Brief

## Project

This is a working prototype for a premium coaching service called Executive OS.

The service helps experienced professionals, executives, founders, consultants and independent experts navigate the impact of AI on work, judgement, identity and professional value.

It is not AI training. It is not generic executive coaching. It is identity navigation and human coaching during AI transition.

## Audience

- Senior professionals, likely aged 45+
- Well educated
- Often time-poor
- First language may not be English
- Comfortable with strategic language, but resistant to hype
- Likely to value discretion, clarity, judgement and professional credibility

## Tone

Use calm, clear, authoritative UK English.

Avoid:

- startup hype
- motivational slogans
- forced urgency
- overly casual idioms
- “broetry”
- dramatic AI panic language
- US-style sales copy

The tone should feel mature, precise, quietly confident and easy to understand for an international professional audience.

## Strategic Positioning

The central idea:

AI can support diagnosis and preparation. It cannot provide the judgement, trust, challenge, confidentiality and sustained accountability that make coaching valuable.

The paid offer should feel valuable because it helps the client understand:

- where their value is exposed
- where their value is defensible
- what AI should automate or augment
- what should remain human-led
- which coaching journey should begin first

## What Needs Improvement

Please review and refine:

- UX flow from first impression to assessment
- information hierarchy
- visual sophistication
- mobile layout
- interaction design
- assessment question presentation
- result state and paid scan transition
- clarity of paid diagnostic value
- smoothness of the booking path
- separation between public customer flow and private review flow

## Important Structure

There are two experiences:

1. `index.html`
   Customer-facing live site. This should feel polished, credible and complete.

2. `review.html`
   Private reviewer workspace. This is only for market testing, pricing feedback and product review. It should not feel like part of the public website.

Do not merge the reviewer workspace back into the live site.

## Design Direction

The current palette is a coral and teal split-complement direction:

- dark teal / near black base
- coral accent `#E34234`
- mauve / soft pink secondary accent `#C71585`
- teal support colour

The design should have some contrastive relationship to `ecm.dev`, while still feeling credible for an executive coaching offer.

The desired feel:

- premium
- minimal
- composed
- high-trust
- editorial
- intelligent
- quietly distinctive

Avoid a generic SaaS dashboard look. Avoid a wellness-coaching aesthetic. Avoid a busy agency landing page.

## Customer Journey Goal

The free snapshot should create enough value and self-recognition that a suitable visitor feels the paid Deep Navigation Scan is a sensible next step.

The journey should move like this:

1. Understand the offer
2. Recognise the human coaching value
3. Select the relevant professional archetype
4. Complete the snapshot
5. Receive a useful result
6. See why the paid scan would go deeper
7. Understand the coaching journey
8. Book a private strategy session

## Review Journey Goal

The private review workspace should help testers evaluate:

- whether the live site is clear
- whether the snapshot is compelling
- whether the paid preview is enough to judge value
- which price point feels credible
- what proof would increase trust

The reviewer page should feel clearly separate from the client site.

## Specific Questions For Claude Design

Please produce:

1. A refined UX and visual design direction.
2. Improvements to the assessment interaction and result state.
3. A smoother transition from free snapshot to paid diagnostic.
4. A clearer distinction between `index.html` and `review.html`.
5. Suggested mobile improvements.
6. Any copy refinements needed for clarity, tone and trust.
7. Updated HTML/CSS/JS if possible.

Preserve the core proposition and audience. Improve the craft.
